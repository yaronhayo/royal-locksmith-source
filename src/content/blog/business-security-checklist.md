## Is Your Business Properly Secured?

Commercial property crime costs businesses billions annually. The good news? Most security vulnerabilities are preventable with proper planning and the right systems.

Use this comprehensive checklist to evaluate and improve your business security.

## Physical Security Checklist

### Entry Points
- [ ] All exterior doors have commercial-grade deadbolts
- [ ] Glass doors have reinforced frames or security film
- [ ] Emergency exits have panic bars and alarms
- [ ] Loading dock doors have heavy-duty locks
- [ ] Windows have locks and/or security bars where appropriate

### Access Control
- [ ] Key inventory is documented and up-to-date
- [ ] Master key system limits access by role
- [ ] Electronic access logs entry/exit times
- [ ] Visitor management system in place
- [ ] Terminated employees' access is revoked immediately

### Lighting
- [ ] All entry points are well-lit
- [ ] Parking areas have adequate lighting
- [ ] Motion-activated lights cover blind spots
- [ ] Interior lights visible from outside after hours

## Technology Security Checklist

### Alarm Systems
- [ ] Intrusion detection covers all entry points
- [ ] Motion sensors in key areas
- [ ] Glass break sensors on vulnerable windows
- [ ] System is monitored 24/7
- [ ] Regular testing and maintenance scheduled

### Surveillance
- [ ] Cameras cover entrances, exits, and sensitive areas
- [ ] Recording system stores minimum 30 days
- [ ] Video quality sufficient for identification
- [ ] Remote viewing capability enabled
- [ ] Signs notify visitors of surveillance

### Safes and Secure Storage
- [ ] Safe rated for contents value
- [ ] Bolted down or weighs 1,000+ lbs
- [ ] Combination changes when employees leave
- [ ] Fire rating appropriate for documents

## Operational Security Checklist

### Employee Protocols
- [ ] Background checks for all employees
- [ ] Clear policies on key/access card handling
- [ ] Procedure for lost keys (rekey vs. replace)
- [ ] Cash handling procedures documented
- [ ] After-hours access protocols established

### Emergency Procedures
- [ ] Locksmith on retainer for emergencies
- [ ] Emergency contact list posted
- [ ] Employees know who to call for lockouts
- [ ] After-break-in procedure documented

### Regular Maintenance
- [ ] Locks inspected quarterly
- [ ] Alarm system tested monthly
- [ ] Access logs reviewed regularly
- [ ] Security audit conducted annually

## Industry-Specific Considerations

### Retail
- High cash volumes require focus on safes, cash drops, and after-hours security.

### Restaurants
- Multiple shifts mean complex access schedules. Electronic access excels here.

### Medical Offices
- HIPAA compliance requires secure storage for patient records.

### Warehouses
- Large footprints need comprehensive camera coverage and heavy-duty locks.
