## Technical Mastery: The Logic of Rolling Codes

When you press the "Unlock" button, a complex exchange of data occurs in milliseconds.

### 1. Rolling Code Synchronization
To prevent "Replay Attacks" (where a thief records your signal and plays it back), modern cars use **Rolling Code** technology.
- **The Logic**: Both the car and the fob have an identical "code hopper" algorithm. Every time you press a button, they both move to the next code in a sequence of billions.
- **The Failure**: If the fob battery dies or if the car enters "Security Lockdown," these codes can drift apart. We use specialized diagnostic tools to "Reseed" the algorithm, bringing the car and fob back into a perfect digital handshake.

### 2. Universal Smart Key Generation (The Future of Locksmithing)

## The "Push-to-Start" Backup Logic

Many drivers panic when their smart key battery dies because they think they can't start the car.
- **Passive Induction**: Every push-to-start car has a fallback. By holding the "Dead" fob directly against the Start button, a tiny antenna in the dash uses "Induction" to power the chip inside the fob, allowing you to start the engine.
